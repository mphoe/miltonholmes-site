The Pig Pen Cipher

Introduction

The pigpen cipher (a.k.a the Freemason's cipher or tic-tac-toe cipher) is a simple substitution cipher where the letters of the alphabet are swapped for symbols. You can see the grid on the right on desktop, or at the bottom of the page on mobile.

The cipher is easy to get to grips with, and it's plenty of fun to send 'enciphered' messages between friends - but it's not been used to hide proper secrets for centuries.

Question: Why do you think nobody uses the PigPen cipher for secrets anymore?
Still, we won't let that stop us having some fun with it. Below you'll be able to create your own Pigpen cipher messages, and then there are some pre-encoded messages waiting to be solved.
